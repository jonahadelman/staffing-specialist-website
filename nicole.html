The six-month plan

Each phase is designed to build on the last. By month six the goal is a TA function with documented process, clear ownership, and a pipeline that accumulates value over time rather than starting from scratch with every new search. I'd like to walk through this with you before we formally kick off so we can align on pacing and priorities.

Month 1
Stabilize
Get organized and establish shared ownership
  • Full req triage and ownership assignment
  • Close ghost reqs, fast-track pending offers
  • Launch all Tier 1 executive searches
  • Establish TA team weekly cadence
Month 2
Standardize
Put a consistent process in place across both teams
  • Tier 1 first candidate slate within 14 days, Tier 2 within 21 days
  • HM alignment on profile, comp, and timeline confirmed before sourcing starts on any req
  • Backup candidate protocol on every active offer
  • Weekly HM update template across all TAs
Month 3
Build pipeline
Open pipeline on the highest-priority gaps
  • MTC program cluster: sequence 15+ systems engineering reqs as one campaign
  • Roanoke operator volume: external sourcing activated, weekly fill targets set
  • HiArc: first submission milestones on all 7 reqs
  • First 30-day report to you
Month 4
Create visibility
Give you and leadership a live view of the portfolio
  • TalentBase live as the portfolio tracking tool for the full team
  • You have a real-time view: req status, stage, candidate, next action
  • No more chasing status updates
Month 5
Accelerate
Build the intelligence layer so every search gets easier
  • TalentBench seeded so every candidate sourced is a permanent ESA asset, not lost when a search closes
  • Exec searches at offer stage or close
  • Internal TA team cross-trained and running independently
Month 6
Deliver
Deliver results and document what we built
  • Zero reqs over 180 days
  • Full TA playbook documented and handed to your team
  • Review with you on what the next six months looks like

6
What works best on your end

There are a few things on the ESA side that have a real impact on how fast we can move. These aren't demands. They're the areas where your support makes the biggest difference. If any of them aren't realistic given how things work internally, I'd rather know that upfront so we can plan around it.

HM availability
An intake call before sourcing starts on any req. Even twenty minutes upfront to align on profile, comp, and timeline saves a lot of time downstream.
Interview panel in 3 days
Panel confirmed within 3 business days of a slate submission if possible. Senior candidates especially tend to move on quickly if scheduling drags.
Feedback in 48 hours
Feedback within 48 hours of interviews where you can manage it. It keeps candidates warm and lets us move without losing momentum.
Offer approvals in 5 days
When an offer is pending approval for more than 5 business days, I'll flag it to you. Req 822 is a good example of what's at stake: 323 days open, offer finally in hand, and it needs to close before the candidate moves on.
Comp transparency upfront
Comp band shared before sourcing begins. It's a short conversation that prevents a much longer one later.
Direct feedback
If a search isn't producing the right profiles, the sooner you tell me the better. Early course corrections are easy. Late ones aren't.
